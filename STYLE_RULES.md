# Proposal Writing Style Rules

Apply these rules to every artifact in this engagement (proposal PDFs,
landing page copy, marketing-os mock copy, day-in-the-life narrative,
one-pager, meeting prep). When in doubt: write like a person, not a
marketing department.

## Forbidden / minimize

### 🚫 Em dashes (—)
The em dash (`—`, U+2014) signals AI-generated copy. Avoid.

**Replace with:**
- A period and a new sentence — best default
- A comma — when the second clause is a quick aside
- "and", "or", "with", "including" — when listing
- A colon — when introducing a list or definition

**OK in narrow cases:**
- A direct quote that already contains one
- A formal range like `Mon–Fri` (use en-dash `–` instead, U+2013)

### 🚫 "AI" as a brag
Don't lead with "AI." We sell systems and outcomes, not buzzwords. AI is the means, not the headline.

- ❌ "AI-powered marketing automation"
- ✅ "A marketing system that drafts your social posts, review replies, and SEO pages from a single foreman text-in"

### 🚫 Construction industry jargon to a non-engineer audience
The reader is often a stakeholder who isn't a contractor. Avoid:
- ❌ "comp set" → ✅ "the competition" / "competitors"
- ❌ "GBP" without first introducing → ✅ "Google Business Profile (GBP)" first time, "GBP" after
- ❌ "Map Pack" → ✅ "the top of the page shows three local businesses"
- ❌ "lockup", "stacked variant", "cropped-iron typography" → ✅ "horizontal", "vertical", "rugged headline type"
- ❌ "type-heavy panels" → ✅ "sections with a lot of text"
- ❌ "Lighthouse 95+" → ✅ delete, or say "fast load times"
- ❌ CMS, schema, NAP, citations → spell out or describe

### 🚫 Marketing-speak / hedging
- ❌ "Lands the brand where its buyers already are"
- ❌ "Buyer intent is highest"
- ❌ "Real estate" (as metaphor)
- ❌ "Synergize", "unlock", "leverage" (as verbs)
- ❌ "Solutions"
- ❌ "Best-in-class", "world-class", "industry-leading"
- ❌ "Compelling story"
- ❌ "Frictionless", "seamless"

## Required / preferred

### ✅ Action verbs
Bullets lead with verbs the reader can map to a deliverable: **Add, Create, Build, Update, Connect, Tune, Ensure, List, Set up, We provide, We run, We engage.**

### ✅ "We" voice for service descriptions
"We provide professional photography." / "We run monthly performance reviews." / "We engage your team."

### ✅ Plain English numbers and time
- "80 years" not "eight decades of operation"
- "15 minutes a day" not "minimal daily time investment"

### ✅ Specifics over abstractions
"Spanish Fork sewer expansion ($2.8M, due May 15)" beats "a relevant local opportunity."

### ✅ Spell out acronyms on first use
First use: "Google Business Profile (GBP)". Subsequent uses: "GBP".
Same for: SOP → standard operating procedures, GC → general contractor (if reader is non-construction).

### ✅ Section heads should signal benefit, not feature
- ❌ "Architecture"
- ✅ "Built to save time and bring in leads"

### ✅ Sentence length
Short. Then medium. Sometimes long, when the rhythm calls for it. Vary.

## Rules of thumb

1. If you read a sentence aloud and it sounds like an AI wrote it, rewrite it.
2. If the reader has to look up a word, replace the word.
3. If a bullet starts with a noun phrase, try starting it with a verb.
4. If two bullets say overlapping things, merge or delete one.
5. Em dashes are the strongest tell. Audit any new copy for them before shipping.
