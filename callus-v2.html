The Philosophy · 金継ぎ Kintsugi
Stronger at the seam.

Kintsugi is the Japanese art of mending broken pottery with gold — honoring the repair instead of hiding it, making the object stronger and more valuable for having been broken. At Callus, every weld is a seam we're proud of. The work doesn't hide the fix — it becomes the proof.

Services

What we do.

From mobile repairs on-site to complex custom fabrication in the shop — we cover the full range of steel and welding work for commercial and public works contractors.

Mobile Welding Repair

We come to you. On-site mobile welding for equipment repair, structural fixes, and emergency calls. Truck-mounted welder, crane, and torch — ready to roll.

Stick Welding (SMAW)

Deep, penetrative welds for heavy structural work. No shielding gas required — ideal for outdoor, field, and high-wind conditions.

MIG Welding (GMAW)

The most versatile process for production and fabrication. Automatic filler feed, clean beads, fast turnaround on custom and repeat work.

TIG Welding (GTAW)

The widest range of material compatibility — steel, aluminum, stainless, chromoly. Precision welds for detailed, spec-sensitive work.

Line Boring

Portable on-site machining. We handle 1.5" to 12" diameter bores with precision — restoring equipment at your location without costly teardown and transport.

OFC-A Cutting

Portable oxyacetylene cutting across material thicknesses. Field-ready for demolition, repair, and custom cuts wherever the job needs it.

The Kintsugi Philosophy · 金継ぎ
Why this matters to us

Kintsugi (金継ぎ) is a 15th-century Japanese art form — the practice of repairing broken pottery with lacquer mixed with gold, making the repair the most beautiful part of the object. The break isn't hidden. It's honored.

For a welding and fabrication shop, the metaphor is the work itself. The weld is the seam. We don't cover the join — we make it the point of pride. Every piece we touch leaves stronger than it arrived.
